Labs

What to get tested — and why

Once-a-year labs to build a baseline you can track over time. Codes included are for Labcorp, covered by most insurance with the correct billing codes (ICD-10).

At your doctor visit, explain that "you're looking to establish a 'normal baseline' that you can track over time." The idea is you want to know which biomarkers are optimal, not just "normal".

Some docs may push back (ego-dependent) but ideally your doctor is open to working with you and your goals. If not, it might be worth trying another doctor who is prevention-focused.

Framing matters. State your goals clearly — longevity, preventative care, spotting early warning signs. If they still question it, say you have a friend who is a 'health nerd' who put this together.

On insurance — they may say some won't be covered. That comes down to the doctor using the correct ICD-10 code. Personally, I've done these labs multiple times a year for four years and haven't paid anything. But mileage may vary.
